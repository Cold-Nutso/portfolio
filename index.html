Colosseum of Punch

Beat up skeletons, get famous

Skeleton ghosts are flooding the Colosseum of Punch! You must fight back, but you can't hit them until they've donned the Reality Crown. Punch as many as you can to rack up your fanbase before you face an inevitable gladiator's death.



This is a really small JavaScript game I programmed. I'd describe it as an arcade-style beat-em-up. You punch the crowned skeleton and watch it ricochet across the screen. The crown falls onto another skeleton ghost and the process repeats. If a crowned skeleton touches you, that's game over. So try and punch as many as possible to get a high score before that happens.


That crown movement was real difficult to accomplish so you better appreciate it. It took a decent amount of physics brain to make that all happen. But the result is totally worth it, it's a great organic visual indicator of which skeleton ghost will wear the crown next.

8-bit Alchemy

Yet another pixel font

SPHINX OF BLACK QUARTZ, JUDGE MY VOW


Despite adamantly hating typography, one day I felt like making a font. It's a cool enough project, but if you don't set limits you could be working on the same font for eternity.


Now this is a pixel font, which I know is a little lame. There are so many out there and they all look identical. Apart from the lowercase letters and some small punctuation, I did try to give the font a unique feel. They have sort of a rune-ish look to them.


I used Fonstruct to make this. I'd recommend it to anyone looking a simple font. If for some reason you'd like to use my font in a project, feel completely free! I'd love to know about it too.

Champion of Worms

Mod character for Blazing Beaks

I created a mod character for Blazing Beaks (developed by Applava). The game is a roguelike bullet-hell that capitalizes on the inherent risk/reward of being greedy. It's ridiculously charming and I can't recommend it enough.


Now let's talk about the Champion of Worms!

It's me. The design is just an exaggerated me. Fortunately, I'm pretty cool, so the prompt makes for a stellar character. The common theme between all Blazing Beaks playable characters is that they have beaks (or bills). I thought I could justify ignoring that rule with lore. He's there to represent every worm so cruelly consumed by avian forces. "Champion of Worms" is an actual title I hold as well, so it all checks out.


I also changed the character's stats to better represent me. He has a really fast rate of fire, an absolutely miniscule amount of health, and a tendency to find extra artifacts. It reflects the way I play; constantly firing, gathering absolutely every item I see, and just assuming I'm not going to get hit.


I don't have a lot of patience for animating so I'm thankful Blazing Beaks went as bare bones as they could with their style. And in addition to a bunch of sprites, I recorded some audio to go along with it. They definitely aren't the pinnacle of voice acting, but it's fun to hear myself in a game.


Mega cheers to Applava for making the mod creation process both simple and intuitive. Without their forward thinking I probably wouldn't have done this.


Here's the mod pack on Steam!

Find Your Poke-Self!

Search Pokémon by height and weight

Here's a JavaScript project that utilizes the Pokémon API. It's a fantastic source of information for every Pokémon in the dex. Literally any information you want about a specific Pokémon can be found here. Super cool.

To find your Pokémon doppelganger, just input your height and weight. The results will come pouring in. If you're having some trouble finding a match, you can edit the leniency of the measurements and find Pokémon that are close enough.

Realistically, there are only going to be a select number of Pokémon any of us will find. Most of them aren't even humanoid, so their height to weight ratios won't be very human either. But practically isn't the point. It's a fun and stupid thing you'll use exactly once.

You can check it out here!
